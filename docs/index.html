Pre-Market Preparation

60 min before market open — Foundation for the entire day

The pre-market phase is where AI earns its keep. Instead of spending 2 hours scanning headlines and drawing levels manually, Claude compresses the information layer so you start the day with a structured, actionable brief.

📡 Step 1A: AI News & Macro Scan AI-Led
  • Global cues check: Ask Claude to summarize overnight action — US indices close, SGX Nifty, Asia open, crude oil, DXY, US yields, any geopolitical flash points.
  • Domestic news filter: Ask Claude to scan for market-moving domestic news — RBI policy signals, sector-specific events, earnings surprises, FII/DII flow data.
  • Sentiment classification: Have Claude classify overall sentiment as Bullish, Bearish, or Neutral with a 1-line reasoning for each.
🤖 Claude Prompt — Morning Brief
You are my trading analyst. Search the web and give me a pre-market brief for NSE trading today: 1. Global overnight summary: US indices (S&P, Nasdaq, Dow), SGX Nifty, crude oil, DXY, US 10Y yield, gold — each with % change and 1-line implication. 2. Asia markets current status. 3. Key domestic news that could move Nifty, Bank Nifty, or specific F&O stocks today. 4. FII/DII flow data from yesterday. 5. Overall sentiment score: Bullish / Neutral / Bearish, with 1-line reasoning. Keep it concise, actionable, and formatted as a table where possible.
Claude AI Web Search
📐 Step 1B: Key Level Identification Hybrid
  • Pull support/resistance from TradingView: Open your Nifty/BankNifty charts. Mark previous day high/low/close, weekly pivots, and any visible S/R zones on 75-min and daily timeframes.
  • AI level validation: Give Claude the previous day's OHLC + current open estimate. Ask it to compute pivot points (Standard, Fibonacci, CPR) and flag confluence zones.
  • Gap analysis: If there's a gap up/down, ask Claude to classify gap type (common, breakaway, exhaustion) based on context and prior trend.
🤖 Claude Prompt — Level Computation
Nifty 50 previous session data: Open: [XXXX], High: [XXXX], Low: [XXXX], Close: [XXXX] Calculate: 1. Standard Pivot Point (PP, R1, R2, R3, S1, S2, S3) 2. Fibonacci Pivot levels 3. Central Pivot Range (CPR) — TC, Pivot, BC 4. Expected opening gap based on SGX Nifty at [XXXX] 5. Key confluence zones where multiple levels overlap Format as a clean table. Flag the 2-3 most important levels I should watch today.
TradingView Claude AI
🎯 Step 1C: Watchlist Build Hybrid
  • TradingView Screener: Run your saved screener filters — stocks near key EMAs (12/34 band), high relative volume, sector leaders/laggards.
  • AI-assisted filtering: Feed the screener output to Claude with your system criteria (e.g., Wavy Tunnel alignment, MTF confluence) and ask it to rank the top 5-7 names by setup quality.
  • Final watchlist: Narrow to max 5 stocks + index. More than that fragments your attention.

⚡ Pre-Market Discipline Rule

  • Do NOT enter any trades during pre-market prep.
  • If you feel FOMO from gap moves, write it down and wait for your system setup.
  • Prep work done? Proceed to Phase 2 only after the opening bell.

Market Open Routine

First 45 minutes — Observe, screen, identify setups

The market open is information-gathering time, not trigger-pulling time. Use the first 15 minutes to observe the opening range form. Then engage AI to filter the noise and surface the best setups from your watchlist.

👁️ Step 2A: Opening Range Observation (First 15 min) Manual
  • No trades for first 15 minutes. Let the opening range (OR) form on a 5-min chart. Mark OR high and OR low on TradingView.
  • Observe gap behavior: Is the gap filling or extending? Is initial volume strong or fading?
  • Note sector rotation: Which sectors are leading (green) and lagging (red)? Bank Nifty vs Nifty relative strength?
  • Check your pre-market levels: Did price open above/below your key CPR or pivot? This updates your bias.

✓ Opening Range Rules

  • OR High/Low = your first intraday reference frame. Trade with the OR breakout direction, not against it.
  • If price is inside the OR after 15 min, wait for a decisive breakout before initiating.
  • Narrow OR (< 0.3% range on Nifty) → expect a directional move. Wide OR (> 0.7%) → range-bound risk.
🔍 Step 2B: AI-Assisted Setup Identification AI-Led
  • Screenshot your watchlist charts (5-min and 75-min views) and send to Claude for pattern recognition — tunnel alignment, EMA band behavior, divergence signals.
  • Ask Claude to classify each setup as: A+ (textbook), B (decent, needs confirmation), or Skip.
  • For top setups: Ask Claude to define the exact entry trigger, stop loss level, and first target — aligned with your system rules.
🤖 Claude Prompt — Setup Scanner
Here are my watchlist charts after the first 15 minutes of market open. [Attach screenshots] For each stock, evaluate: 1. Is the 12/34 EMA band bullish, bearish, or neutral on the 5-min and 75-min? 2. Is there a valid entry setup forming? (Breakout, pullback to EMA, pattern completion) 3. Where is the logical stop loss (below recent swing low or EMA band)? 4. What's the first target (next resistance / 1:2 RR)? Rate each as A+ (take immediately), B (wait for confirmation), or Skip. Only show me the A+ and B setups.
📋 Step 2C: Trade Plan Lock-In Manual
  • From the A+ setups: Write your trade plan for each — ticker, direction, entry, SL, T1, T2, position size (use the calculator in the Tools tab).
  • Set TradingView alerts at your entry trigger levels so you don't have to stare at charts.
  • Pre-define your max risk budget: Total capital at risk across all open positions should not exceed 2% of your trading capital.
Key principle: If you don't have a written plan before entering, you don't have an edge — you have a gamble. Write it down or type it to Claude. Verbalizing forces clarity.

Active Trading Phase

Core session — Execute, monitor, manage with AI guardrails

This is where you execute planned trades and manage live positions. AI's role shifts to monitoring, rule-checking, and preventing emotional drift. You drive; AI is your co-pilot.

⚡ Step 3A: Trade Execution Protocol Manual
  • Entry confirmation: Only enter when your pre-defined trigger fires AND you can articulate why in one sentence. "It broke above OR high with volume on a bullish EMA stack" — that's a valid reason.
  • Immediate SL placement: Stop loss goes in the system the moment you enter. Not mental — actual bracket/SL-M order.
  • Position sizing: Use the calculator (Tools tab) — never size by gut feeling.
  • Log the trade instantly: Ticker, time, direction, entry, SL, target, lot size, rationale — in your trade log (Notion/Sheets).

🚨 Hard Rules — Non-Negotiable

  • Max 1% risk per trade (2% absolute maximum for A+ setups only)
  • Max 3 simultaneous open positions
  • Max daily loss = 3% of capital → STOP trading for the day
  • No revenge trades — if you hit 2 consecutive losses, take a 30-min break
  • No averaging down on losing positions — ever
🤖 Step 3B: AI Monitoring & Rule Checks AI-Led
  • Mid-trade validation: If you're tempted to move your stop, widen your position, or take a trade not on your plan — ask Claude first.
  • Sentiment shift detection: Every 60-90 minutes, ask Claude to check if any breaking news or macro shift has changed the market context from your pre-market thesis.
  • Pattern exit signals: If your system generates potential exit signals (divergence, trend exhaustion), screenshot the chart and ask Claude to validate.
🤖 Claude Prompt — Emotional Check / Rule Guard
I'm in a trade and feeling the urge to [move my stop / add to my position / take an unplanned trade]. Here's the situation: Stock: [Ticker], Direction: [Long/Short] Entry: [price], Current SL: [price], Current price: [price] Original plan: [1-line summary] What I want to do now: [describe the urge] Based on sound risk management principles, should I follow through or stick to my original plan? Be brutally honest.
🎚️ Step 3C: Position Management Framework Hybrid

✅ Winners — Trail & Protect

  • At 1R profit → move SL to breakeven
  • At 1.5R → trail SL to lock in 0.5R
  • At 2R → book 50% position, trail rest
  • At 3R → book another 25%, trail rest with EMA

❌ Losers — Cut & Move On

  • SL hit → exit immediately, no hoping
  • Time stop: if no movement in 30 min → reassess
  • If thesis invalidated → exit even before SL
  • Never widen the SL after entry — never
📊 Step 3D: Real-Time AI Signals Layer AI-Led
  • TradingView alerts as triggers: Set Pine Script alerts for your system signals (EMA crossovers, RSI divergence, volume spikes). When they fire, validate with Claude before acting.
  • Sector rotation monitoring: Ask Claude every 90 minutes to check which sectors are gaining/losing relative strength. Rotate your attention accordingly.
  • Volatility regime check: If India VIX spikes > 5% intraday, ask Claude to assess whether this changes your position sizing or strategy.
🤖 Claude Prompt — Signal Validation
My TradingView alert fired for [TICKER] on [timeframe]. Signal: [describe — e.g., "bullish EMA crossover on 5-min with RSI above 60"]. Here's the chart screenshot. [Attach] Validate this signal: 1. Does it align with the higher timeframe trend (75-min)? 2. Is there confirmation from volume? 3. Any divergence or exhaustion signals that contradict? 4. Suggested entry, SL, and target if valid.

Mid-Day Review

~30 min — Recalibrate bias and assess performance

The mid-day review is your circuit breaker. Markets shift, emotions accumulate, and your morning thesis may no longer hold. This phase uses AI to force an objective recalibration before the afternoon session.

📊 Step 4A: P&L Snapshot Manual
  • Tally your P&L so far. Realized and unrealized. How does it compare to your daily target and max loss limit?
  • Count your trades. How many taken vs planned? Any unplanned trades? Were those winners or losers?
  • Emotional check-in: On a scale of 1-5, how disciplined have you been? Any revenge trades, FOMO entries, or premature exits?
🔄 Step 4B: AI Bias Recalibration AI-Led
  • Market structure update: Ask Claude to assess if the morning trend is intact or if the market character has changed (trending → ranging, or vice versa).
  • News update: Any mid-day developments that shift the thesis? Central bank statements, geopolitical events, large block deals?
  • Afternoon game plan: Based on updated context, should you be aggressive, defensive, or flat?
🤖 Claude Prompt — Mid-Day Recalibration
Mid-day review for my trading session: Morning bias was: [Bullish/Bearish/Neutral] Trades taken: [X], Winners: [X], Losers: [X] Current P&L: [₹XXXX] Emotional state: [Focused / Frustrated / Overconfident / Neutral] Current Nifty: [XXXX], BankNifty: [XXXX] Any notable mid-day developments? [Check web] Based on this, advise: 1. Is my morning bias still valid or should I flip/go neutral? 2. Should I continue trading aggressively or scale down? 3. Any new setups forming for the afternoon session? 4. If I'm near my daily loss limit, tell me to stop — don't sugarcoat it.
⚖️ Step 4C: Decision Matrix Manual

🟢 Continue Trading If:

  • P&L is positive or within normal drawdown
  • Emotional state is calm and focused
  • Market structure supports further trades
  • You have remaining A/B setups on your list

🔴 STOP Trading If:

  • Hit daily loss limit (3% of capital)
  • 3+ consecutive losing trades
  • Feeling frustrated, angry, or revenge-driven
  • Market is choppy with no clear direction

End-of-Day Wrap-Up

Post-close — AI-powered journaling & continuous improvement

This is the highest-ROI phase most traders skip. AI-powered journaling transforms raw trade data into patterns, lessons, and actionable improvements. Done consistently, this compounds your edge over weeks and months.

📝 Step 5A: Trade Log Completion Manual
  • Complete every field in your trade log: entry/exit time, entry/exit price, SL, target, position size, P&L (₹ and R-multiple), setup grade (A/B/C).
  • Add screenshots of entry and exit charts for each trade. These are gold for future review.
  • Tag each trade: System (followed rules), Impulse (deviated from plan), Missed (valid setup you didn't take).
🤖 Step 5B: AI-Powered Trade Review AI-Led
  • Feed your trade log to Claude and ask for a structured analysis. The AI can spot patterns you're blind to — recurring mistakes, time-of-day biases, setup-type performance differences.
  • Ask Claude to grade your discipline on a 1-10 scale based on your rule adherence today.
  • Request a "one thing to fix tomorrow" — a single, specific improvement point for the next session.
🤖 Claude Prompt — EOD Journal Analysis
Here's my complete trade log for today: [paste or attach] Analyze my session: 1. Win rate, average R per trade, total R earned/lost 2. Best trade: what made it work? 3. Worst trade: what went wrong? Was it a system trade or impulse? 4. Pattern observations: any recurring mistakes or strengths? 5. Time analysis: which time window were my best/worst trades? 6. Discipline score (1-10): did I follow my rules? 7. One specific thing to improve tomorrow. Be honest and constructive. Don't just tell me what I want to hear.
📈 Step 5C: Weekly Pattern Mining (Every Friday) AI-Led
  • End of each week: Send Claude the full week's trade log and ask for a deeper statistical analysis — equity curve shape, setup performance breakdown, emotional pattern detection.
  • Compare against previous weeks: Is your edge improving, degrading, or flat? Is there a specific setup type you should trade more/less?
  • System refinement suggestions: Ask Claude if any rule modifications or new filters would have improved your results this week.
🤖 Claude Prompt — Weekly Review
Here are my daily trade logs for this week (Mon-Fri): [paste all] Generate a weekly trading report: 1. Overall stats: total trades, win rate, avg winner vs avg loser, expectancy (avg R), max drawdown 2. Best day and worst day — what was different? 3. Setup performance breakdown: which setup types are profitable and which are bleeding? 4. Emotional/discipline trend across the week 5. Time-of-day analysis: when am I sharpest? 6. Compared to last week [paste last week's summary if available], am I improving? 7. Top 3 actionable changes for next week, ranked by impact. Format as a structured report with clear sections.
🔄 Step 5D: Next-Day Prep Seed Hybrid
  • Overnight holds: If carrying any positional trades, note the plan for tomorrow — trail levels, exit triggers, or expiry considerations for F&O.
  • Preliminary watchlist: Based on today's movers and sector rotation, ask Claude to suggest 3-5 stocks to watch tomorrow.
  • Macro calendar: Ask Claude to flag any major events tomorrow — US data releases, earnings, RBI announcements, F&O expiry.
  • Set intention: Write one sentence: "Tomorrow I will focus on [X]" — based on today's improvement point.
